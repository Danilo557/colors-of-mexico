Mark M. Palmer

Merida is one great city

There are many terrific cities to visit in Mexico. Merida is certainly one of them. It has a great central plaza with much of what you would want to see within walking distance, tons of hotels of varying degree of price and quality, lots of restaurants and shops, many governmental buildings and museums worth visiting, theatres, and musical performances that are frequently held and sometimes right on the streets surrounding the central plaza. Sunday is a special day in the center. Go in the morning for food and more fun.

If you will be there for a while take the free tour of the central plaza area that starts from one of the tourist offices on the square at 9:30 on weekday mornings. It will be a great start to your stay in Merida. The city is close enough to Mayan sites like Uxmal and Chichen Itza to make it a center for visiting many such places plus it is close to the Gulf of Mexico places like Progresso. There are several tour companies who will arrange for you to get to these other places or you can go on your own.

Puebla; an undiscovered treasure

Many of Mexico's colonial cities have unmistakable charms and are reasonably well-known by travellers. Better-known places like Morelia and Queretero have lively town squares, beautiful Spanish-colonial architecture and tasty regional cooking. Even Cuernevaca has lots to offer despite its constant, fast-moving traffic. Puebla, though known at least by some for its Talavera tiles, is less well-known for its myriad other attractions. When I was trying to research Puebla for a trip there last winter (2007) I could find very little that actually gave me a flavour of the place. I've since learned that Puebla's tourist authorities are only slowly beginning to offer information in English. And it's also the case that if you go there few of its citizens will speak any English. To me that didn't matter. I wanted to brush up on my limited Spanish and discover a new city.

Benji Stawski

Great for families

After much anticipation, the Hilton Cancun, an All-Inclusive Resort started welcoming guests in November 2021. It opened as a part of a series of new Hilton hotels being developed in the area, including the adjacent Waldorf Astoria Cancun (opening in September), the Conrad Tulum Riviera Maya and the Hilton Tulum Riviera Maya, an All-Inclusive Resort.

I was particularly excited for this hotel to open as the renderings looked incredible, resembling a higher-end Conrad hotel more than a typical Hilton. Of course, finished products don’t always live up to expectations, so I wanted to experience the new resort firsthand and a recent trip to Mexico was the perfect opportunity for me to do so. My stay would be about five months after the property first opened its doors, giving it plenty of time to iron out any new-hotel kinks.
