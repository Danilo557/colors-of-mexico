Playa del carmen

Playa del Carmen is the heartbeat of the Mayan Riviera. The Mexican Caribbean, which makes this destination the ideal gateway for domestic and foreign travelers, who are seduced by its beaches, cenotes (deep natural wells or sinkholes) and exclusive resorts.

Town Playa del carmen

Downtown restaurants await, for a culinary experience that ranges from traditional Mayan cuisine to international fare. Afterward, you can tour its most emblematic street, Fifth Avenue, well-known for its boutiques, handicraft stores, and bars.

Exclusive Open Cenote

These impressive sinkholes are formed after the collapse of limestone bedrock connecting the surface with an aquatic world of calcareous caves or subterranean rivers, some of them are great places for swimming and relax.

Cholula's Piramid

This is the most important pre-Hispanic settlement of those explored so far in the state of Puebla, and one of the main sites in Mexico. Its attractions include the Great Pyramid and the plaza known as the “courtyard of altars.”

Skyview Zacatlan

Enjoy an impressive view of the Puebla’s mountains from this glass viewpoint that will make you feel among the clouds.

Zócalo de Puebla

The main square of Puebla de los Ángeles is as old as the city itself, created in 1531 when the city was laid out, becoming from then on the nerve center of the city due to its central location.

Arqueologica Zone Teotihuacan

Nahuatl: Teōtihuácān, "Place where the gods were made; city of the gods". Is the name given to what was one of the largest cities in Mesoamerica during pre-Hispanic times. The toponym is of Nahuatl origin and was used by the Mexicas, but the name given by its inhabitants is unknown.

Catedral CDMX

The Metropolitan Cathedral is one of the most important temples in America, its majestic proportions make it a masterpiece.
